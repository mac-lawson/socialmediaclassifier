About Pixelizer


Pixelizer aims to moderate the vast ocean of content on social media today through machine learning

The idea is to classify posts into different categories, which is done differently from text to image posts.

Text posts are classified through sentiment analysis, which essentially describes a post as positive, negative or neutral. For example, a positive post may be "I loved going to Spain!".

Meanwhile, a neutral post would be entail "What are the best places to visit in Spain?" or "I had a trip to Spain last month." And of course, an example of a negative post would be "I hate Spain."

An AI model is trained to recognize the sentiment of posts through hundreds of thousands of tweets, and reinforced by human feedback.

Eventually, the AI is able to detect the sentiment of posts to a high degree of accuracy. A similar process is used for image posts.

Here, we first classified the subject of the image through a pretrained model from the Hugging Face pipeline. Then, we developed the model to give a sentiment of the subject of the image, classifying it as safe, neutral (but potentially requiring age limiting) or dangerous.

Through human fine tuning (which you can try yourself

here

) the model was trained to high accuracy. Of course, it isn't perfect, but given the time constraints of the project, it would not have been feasible to fine tune further.



We hope this project demonstrates the accessibility and utility of AI in that even high schoolers can create an algorithm that could be used in the most popular social media apps. As well as the infinite possibilities of AI. Classification is just one possible use of this model. It could also be used to recommend similar topics, or find demographics for areas of interest.

You can submit your own images on the Home page. Only .jpg images are supported for now. Enjoy!
